Important Guidelines

Safety & Ethics

MedEcho AI is a strictly educational simulation platform. Please read our safety guidelines before practicing.

1. Strictly Educational Use

MedEcho AI is designed exclusively for educational training and OSCE exam preparation. It is not a medical device, nor is it a clinical decision support tool. It must never be used to diagnose, treat, or offer medical advice for real human beings.

2. No Real Patient Data (PHI/PII)

To comply with HIPAA, GDPR, and global medical ethics standards, you must never input real patient data, identifiable case histories, or protected health information (PHI) into the MedEcho AI platform. All interactions are processed through third-party AI models (OpenAI/ElevenLabs) and are intended for fictional simulation only.

3. AI Hallucinations & Limitations

While MedEcho AI is built upon state-of-the-art Large Language Models, AI is prone to "hallucinations" (producing plausible but medically incorrect information). Users should cross-reference medical facts, management plans, and guidelines generated by the platform with official, peer-reviewed medical literature (e.g., NICE guidelines, BMJ).

4. Generated Investigations

Any images (X-rays, MRIs, clinical photos) or lab reports generated during a consultation are synthetic and educational. They may lack the nuanced accuracy required for real-world radiological or pathological interpretation and are provided solely to test communication and diagnostic reasoning within the simulated environment.
